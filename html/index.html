About Me

I'm currently a master student majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Pittsburgh. My advisor is Professor Zhi-Hong Mao. I obtained my Bachelor of Science degree from the School of Mathematical Sciences at the Ocean University of China , under the guidance of Professor Yong Zhao. My research interests lie in leveraging the rigor of mathematics to enhance the performance of machine learning algorithms. My recent research project is focused on Topological Data Analysis, Dimensionality Reduction, Manifold Learning, and Sleep Stages Classification.

The way I see myself

I am a quintessential ENTJ—outgoing, a natural leader who thrives in the company of friends, yet finds equal comfort in the quietude of solitude. What gives me the deepest sense of peace is witnessing my own progress, step by step, whether in academics or life. It is this feeling of forward motion that fuels my determination in the face of challenges.
My love for reading allows me to explore the vast richness of the world without ever leaving my home, while soccer serves as my outlet for stress. Every time I step onto the field, with the wind rushing past my ears, it feels as if time itself slows down. In those moments, life’s worries are left behind. Soccer is more than just a hobby for me—it’s a source of inspiration. My idol, Lionel Messi, motivates me with every step he takes, reminding me that no matter how difficult the journey, perseverance and unwavering effort will carry you through.
The challenges I’ve faced extend far beyond academics. At one point, I was overweight, with a body fat percentage of 28%, and I faced the sting of rejection from someone I admired. Yet, over the course of just four months, I lost 20 kilograms and reduced my body fat to 15%.
My life is much like a game of soccer—setbacks and challenges fuel my drive, and progress is the greatest source of my peace. Every challenge, no matter how big or small, makes me more resilient, and fills me with hope for the future.
